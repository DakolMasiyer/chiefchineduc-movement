1

🛣️ Infrastructure First

Signature: The Okigwe Road Restoration Bill

Fix the roads. Restore movement. Reconnect communities. This is not about patching potholes — it is about legislating Okigwe's infrastructure debt into a federal obligation.

  • Introduce the Okigwe Road Restoration Bill within 90 days of swearing-in
  • Target Okigwe–Enugu corridor rehabilitation as first visible deliverable
  • Market access roads connecting 5 major rural communities
  • Quarterly public accountability tracking published online
  • Federal road advocacy through Senate Infrastructure Committee
The Differentiator

"Not 'I will advocate.' — 'I will table a bill and name it.' A bill with a name creates accountability that vague advocacy cannot."

2

⚡ Power & Energy Access

Signature: The Okigwe Electrification Initiative

Darkness is both literal and symbolic in Okigwe. Electricity is not a utility request — it is a dignity issue.

  • Community solar pilot project delivered before election day
  • Collect 50,000 signatures on the Okigwe Electrification Petition
  • Partner with NERC and Imo State on hybrid solar grid for 3 wards
  • Push for Okigwe's inclusion in Nigeria's Rural Electrification Programme
  • Support independent community power cooperatives
The Differentiator

"Deliver one community solar project before the vote — not after. That is proof, not promise."

3

💼 Jobs & Local Economy

Signature: The Okigwe Trades & Skills Fund

Chief Chukwuonye has already put machines in young people's hands. The Trades & Skills Fund formalises what he has already been doing.

  • 500 youth trained in technical skills before election day — with names and faces documented
  • The Okigwe Trades & Skills Fund formally launched pre-election
  • SME market fairs in all 3 LGAs to activate local trade
  • Agro-processing support for farming communities
  • Apprenticeship matching linking youth with local business owners
The Differentiator

"His past work IS the proof. The campaign names it, shows it, and scales it visibly. Every opponent must run on promises. He runs on proof."

4

🛡️ Security & Community Safety

Signature: Okigwe Community Watch Alliance

Security in southeast Nigeria is inseparable from trust. The Watch Alliance positions leadership as someone who builds structures, not just makes statements.

  • Formally establish and fund the Okigwe Community Watch Alliance across all LGAs
  • Advocate for a dedicated federal security deployment to Okigwe Zone
  • Community-based intelligence network involving local leaders and youth groups
  • Quarterly security town halls — accountability in public, not behind closed doors
  • Partnership with state and federal security agencies
The Differentiator

"An alliance with a name is harder to ignore than a promise to 'collaborate with agencies.' Structure creates accountability."

5

💧 Water & Basic Standards

Signature: Clean Water for Every Ward

Water access is Okigwe's most visible symbol of neglect — and its most achievable early win. Making this tangible before the election turns policy into lived experience.

  • 10 boreholes commissioned in underserved wards before election day
  • Partner with RUWASSA on rural water access programming
  • Integrate water access with sanitation and hygiene campaigns
  • Target market and school communities first for maximum visibility
  • Advocacy for Okigwe's inclusion in the National Water Programme
The Differentiator

"10 boreholes commissioned before election day. Visible, photographable, verifiable. Not after the vote — before."

6

🏥 Healthcare Access

Signature: The Okigwe Health Equity Bill

Healthcare distance kills. The Okigwe Health Equity Bill turns this from an emotional appeal into a legislative obligation.

  • Introduce the Okigwe Health Equity Bill within first 100 days in the Senate
  • Advocate for upgrading Okigwe General Hospital to a Federal Medical Centre
  • Mobile health units serving communities more than 15km from a facility
  • Emergency referral support fund for critical cases
  • Maternal and child health programme targeting rural communities
The Differentiator

"A bill with a name creates accountability. Vague advocacy does not. The Health Equity Bill gives constituents something to point to and hold their Senator against."

7

🎓 Youth, Education & Opportunity

Signature: Stay Home, Build Okigwe Programme

Youth migration is Okigwe's silent crisis. "Stay Home, Build Okigwe" is not just a programme name — it is the entire message of the campaign reframed for the next generation.

  • Launch "Stay Home, Build Okigwe" as a branded scholarship and skills programme
  • 100 scholarship awards to Okigwe students in STEM and vocational programmes
  • Digital and tech training hubs in 2 LGA headquarters
  • Apprenticeship matching connecting youth with local business owners
  • Reduce youth migration pressure through investment in local opportunity
The Differentiator

"The name 'Stay Home, Build Okigwe' IS the message. Youth hear it and understand the ask immediately. A programme name that is also a movement slogan."
